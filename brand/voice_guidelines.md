# Brand Voice Guidelines

This document defines the brand voice for ShaMstack. All content created by agents should adhere to these guidelines.

---

## Tone

**Conversational yet authoritative**

- Write like you're explaining to a smart colleague
- No corporate jargon or buzzwords
- Direct and specific, not vague

**Example:**
- Good: "Most engineering teams measure the wrong things."
- Bad: "In today's fast-paced digital landscape, organizations must leverage cutting-edge solutions."

---

## Do

- **Use real examples** — Reference actual companies, metrics, scenarios
- **Lead with data** — Back up claims with numbers or sources
- **Acknowledge tradeoffs** — Nothing is perfect, be honest about limitations
- **Write for scanners** — Short paragraphs, clear headings, bullet points
- **Use active voice** — "We built" not "Was built by us"

---

## Don't

- **No filler phrases** — "In today's world," "It's important to note"
- **No buzzwords** — "Leverage," "synergy," "best-in-class," "robust"
- **No unsubstantiated claims** — "3x improvement" needs a source
- **No competitor bashing** — Fair comparisons okay, attacks not
- **No jargon without explanation** — Define acronyms first use

---

## Reading Level

Target: **Grade 8-10** for B2B content

- Simple words over complex ones
- Short sentences (15-25 words avg)
- Clear structure

---

## Forbidden Phrases

- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Best-in-class"
- "Robust"
- "Comprehensive"
- "Nuanced"
- "Game-changer"
- "Disruptive"
- "Synergy"
- "Leverage" (as a verb)

---

## Example Passages

### Good Example

```
Most engineering teams measure the wrong things.

Lines of code. Velocity points. Story points completed.

These are vanity metrics. They feel productive but don't predict success.

After analyzing 200+ engineering teams, here are the 5 metrics that actually matter:

1. Cycle Time — How fast does work move from commit to deploy?
2. Deployment Frequency — How often do you ship to production?
3. Change Failure Rate — What % of deploys cause incidents?
4. Time to Recovery — How fast do you bounce back from failures?
5. Developer Satisfaction — Are your engineers thriving or burning out?

The teams that track these outperform by 3x.
```

### Bad Example

```
In today's rapidly evolving technological landscape, organizations must leverage 
cutting-edge engineering metrics to achieve best-in-class performance. Our 
comprehensive, robust solution provides nuanced insights into your team's 
synergistic capabilities, enabling game-changing improvements in productivity.
```

---

## Formatting

- **Headings** — Use H1 → H2 → H3 hierarchy
- **Paragraphs** — 2-4 sentences max
- **Lists** — Use bullets for related items, numbered for sequences
- **Bold** — Use sparingly for emphasis
- **Links** — Descriptive anchor text, not "click here"

---

## CTA Guidelines

- **Be direct** — "Download the template" not "Click here to download"
- **State value** — What do they get?
- **Low friction** — "Free template" not "Request a demo"
